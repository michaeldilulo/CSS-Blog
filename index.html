Software Developer Bootcamp

Initial Entry: Prior to class

"Hardware: The only part of a computer that you can KICK"

In September of 2019, I was doing some research in Software Development. I have always wanted to get into this field I thought. Just a few weeks before I had spoken to one of my buddies who went through the course and he really enjoyed it. I was incredibly excited to start this journey. I made the phone call and got in touch with their Recruiter, who directed me to the CEO for the whiteboard (Technical challenge). Now, I walked in, ready to rock and roll thinking I had grasped the concept of programming through all the Udemy videos I had watched. The CEO took me into a room with the awful dreadful whiteboard, and asked me to solve a challenge, that I could with ease now, but had some hiccups along the way. The one thing that really saved my butt and got me into this course, was the ability to explain what I was thinking and it made sense. It was on that day I was officially accepted into the program to start 4 November 2019. Still, to this day, although I have not found a job yet, with COVID 19 taking off, was hands down the best decision I have made. This course for me started right after my drill weekend, and for those individuals out there who feel like they cannot balance it, trust me it can be done.

During my time preparing for the bootcamp, I was directed to use the courses on Free Code Camp and the different sections. This site at the time was non user friendly and really didn't teach a whole lot as far as programming goes. It wasn't until the middle of my cohort that the course was upgraded and made it a much better user experience. Now (September) I have actually gone into Free Code Camp, completed the first 4/5 projects and still use it for a good reference. I definitely recommend using Free Code Camp now for preparation before starting to get into a bootcamp. The second thing I would recommend is going to https://www.udemy.com and picking up a course on there that's based off full stack web developement. Just to see and have an idea of how things flow. The big take away from this is, if you decide to go with a full stack course on Udemy, ensure that it is a vue.js full stack application course with node.js and MongoDB. Basically building MEVN projects. Also, you need to do your research on the MVC(Model-View-Controller) pattern, because while the first week might seem like a breeze, MVC is a foreign concept to people and it can make the course really difficult. In time you will grasp it because you will use it everyday from then on out, but it is really important to see if you can understand the pattern the best you can before entering into the course. Stay tuned as I will talk about my experience through this bootcamp with as much as I can recollect from it. I should have started this back when I went through the course, but in Full Stack Development, you are busy, programming everyday. So you will have to excuse me if any of the content is fuzzy with the details.
